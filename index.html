How does this work?

The concept behind this is simple: take your salary, subtract your expenses, keep a little in your bank account, and invest the rest. If you ever have an emergency or large expense in life, you can easily take out from your investments.

The magic is in compound interest—your money earns money, and that money earns more money. Starting sooner gives your investments decades to grow exponentially.

You can do this with any salary, the main factor is how much you spend. We also have different modes for returns and risk appetites, so you can customize your budget to your liking.

Return Models

"Underestimate" uses conservative returns (9.5% stocks, 2% bonds) - the safe option to guarantee your minimum outcome. "Current Rates" uses recent average returns (11.5% stocks, 4% bonds).

What's this? Return models determine how much growth your investments might experience annually.

Current Rates: Based on recent historical returns (11.5% for stocks, 4% for bonds). This is more optimistic but reflects market performance in recent decades.

Underestimate: Uses more conservative estimates (9.5% for stocks, 2% for bonds). This builds in a safety margin to account for potential market downturns.

Risk Appetite

How much risk? Aggressive Growth = 80% stocks/20% bonds, Moderate = 70% stocks/25% bonds/5% cash, Passive = 60% stocks/35% bonds/5% cash.

What's this? Risk appetite determines how you allocate your investments between stocks, bonds, and cash.

Aggressive Growth (80% stocks): Not actually "risky" if you have a long time horizon. Higher stock allocation means more short-term volatility but typically higher long-term returns.

Moderate (70% stocks): A balanced approach with good growth potential and some stability from bonds and cash.

Passive (60% stocks): More conservative approach with less volatility but potentially lower long-term returns.
